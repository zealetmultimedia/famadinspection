Eaves Fascia Corrosion & Wall Moisture Ingress

Severely corroded eaves fascia and downpipe at the gable end. Extensive efflorescence, paint delamination, and moisture staining on the external wall below the eaves indicate chronic water penetration.

F — Defect 02
Widespread Biological Fouling & Eaves Corrosion

Heavy algae and moss growth covering the full extent of the roof surface, retaining moisture and accelerating sheet deterioration. Eaves fascia exhibits significant rust corrosion. Wall below eaves shows efflorescence and paint failure.

Medium Severity
F — Defect 03
Multiple Translucent Sheet Patch Repairs

Aerial view reveals numerous translucent polycarbonate or fibreglass sheets interspersed among the existing corrugated asbestos sheets, indicating a history of repeated leak repairs. The pattern of patching across multiple bays suggests systemic sheet failure.

Medium Severity
F — Defect 04
Screw Corrosion, Rust Streaking & Ponding Water

Close-up of roof surface showing multiple vertical rust streaks running from corroded fixing screws down the sheet profile. Active ponding water visible across the sheet surface, indicating inadequate drainage falls. Corrosion has penetrated through the sheet coating at fixing points.

F — Defect 05
Parapet Coping Failure & Severe Biological Growth

The metal fascia coping cap at the parapet/gable junction is delaminating and pulling away from the wall, exposing the structure to water ingress. Severe algae and biological growth covers the parapet face. This condition allows direct water entry at the wall–roof junction.

Aerial view of the clerestory monitor strip shows multiple broken and missing glass panes along the full run of the ventilation monitor. Defect Classification Note

Severity ratings above are indicative and based on visual inspection. A formal structural assessment should be commissioned to determine remediation priority. High Severity defects require urgent attention and may affect structural integrity or weather-tightness. Medium Severity defects require scheduled maintenance within the inspection period. Low Severity defects should be monitored and addressed during routine maintenance.

Defect descriptions and severity ratings should be updated by the responsible engineer following detailed close-up inspection. This register documents the defects observed during the aerial and ground-level photographic survey only.

Project Background

About This Inspection

FAMAD PLC operates a large-scale production facility with extensive roof coverage spanning 27,836 m² across 20 structures on a 56,819 m² site at Kilometer 15, Ikorodu Road, Ojota, Lagos. Due to the size, height, and structural complexity of the roofs, traditional manual inspection methods are time consuming, unsafe, and prone to inaccuracies.


This project proposes the use of drone-based aerial inspection and measurement to accurately determine the total roof coverage area, assess roof condition, and provide data required for general maintenance planning and roof replacement cost estimation. The aerial photogrammetric survey enables precise dimensional capture of all structures without the safety risks and access constraints of conventional ground-level or scaffold-based inspection.

Environmental Context

Accelerated Deterioration Risk

The proximity of the FAMAD PLC facility to the Olusosun landfill — one of the largest in West Africa — creates an unusually aggressive corrosion environment that significantly shortens the serviceable life of all metallic roofing components compared to typical industrial facilities.

SO₂
Sulphur dioxide from landfill decomposition accelerates oxidation of metal roof sheets and fixings
H₂S
Hydrogen sulphide causes pitting corrosion on zinc-coated and galvanised surfaces
80%
Relative humidity in Lagos promotes biological growth and accelerates electrochemical corrosion processes
C3–C4
ISO 9223 corrosivity classification. Replacement cycles should be planned accordingly — typically 15–20 years for pre-painted steel
Recommendation: All replacement roofing materials should be specified to a minimum PVDF/Kynar 500 pre-painted coating with a minimum 40-micron dry film thickness, or Zincalume/Galvalume substrate, to maximise service life in this corrosive environment. Standard galvanised sheet without protective coating is not recommended for this site.
